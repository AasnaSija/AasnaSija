# Hi, I'm Aasna Sijapati

I'm a researcher and policy analyst pursuing an **MA in International Development Policy** at Georgetown University's McCourt School of Public Policy (expected May 2026). My work sits at the intersection of gender, labor markets, and international development — with a focus on rigorous quantitative methods and actionable policy insights.

My experience designing education programs for migrant workers in the UAE first sparked my interest in remittances as a tool for development. I'm now focused on how financial and digital innovations — and increasingly, AI — can improve the efficiency and equity of cross-border money transfers, and what that means for households navigating migration and economic precarity.

---

## Research Interests

- **Remittances & migration** — labor market outcomes, skill transitions, diaspora financial flows
- **Gender & development** — childcare markets, maternal labor, female human development outcomes
- **Impact evaluation** — randomized experiments, causal inference, heterogeneous treatment effects
- **Financial inclusion** — digital payments, cross-border transfers, household welfare
